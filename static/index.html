// Purpose [-]
I homebrew everything — beer, electronics, software, ideas, and apparently, internet archives. Some people collect stamps; I collect knowledge. This is one of my hobbies, and I intend to keep it running for as long as I'm alive.

Digital knowledge is fragile. Websites go dark, servers get decommissioned, links rot, and entire libraries of human work vanish without warning. I've watched too many valuable resources disappear — forums that answered questions nobody else could, hardware manuals for machines that built the modern world, magazines that captured decades of computing history. So I decided to do something about it, one wget at a time.

This project follows the same tradition that drives public libraries, the Internet Archive, and countless community-run mirrors around the world: information belongs to everyone, and it deserves to outlive the servers that first hosted it. No monetization, no ads — just a hobbyist with a Raspberry Pi and a stubborn belief that the internet's collective memory shouldn't depend on whether someone keeps paying a hosting bill.
// Infrastructure [+]
This vault runs on a single Raspberry Pi 4 consuming under 5W of power, served over a residential internet connection. No cloud, no CDN origin, no SLA — when the power goes out, the site goes with it. All data is rsynced across two hard drives — one hot (actively serving) and one cold (offline backup) — so even if a drive fails, nothing is lost. Not enterprise-grade, but enough to sleep at night. Preservation doesn't require a data center, just determination, a spare ARM board, and two hard drives.
